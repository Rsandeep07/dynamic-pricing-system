# Dynamic Surge Pricing Analysis

This project focuses on analyzing surge pricing behavior in dynamic systems
where prices fluctuate based on real-time demand and supply conditions.
The objective is to understand the key drivers behind surge price variations
and extract insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Problem Statement
Dynamic pricing systems are widely used in real-world platforms to manage
demandâ€“supply imbalances. Sudden demand spikes, limited supply, time-based
patterns, and external factors such as weather and location can lead to
price surges. Understanding these drivers is critical for building fair
and efficient pricing strategies.

---

## Project Objectives
- Analyze how demand and supply levels influence surge pricing
- Study the impact of time, weather, and location on surge multipliers
- Identify patterns associated with abnormal price increases
- Generate insights that can support automated pricing or decision systems

---

## Workflow
- Data Cleaning and Validation
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Feature-Level Analysis of Pricing Drivers
- Surge Multiplier Analysis
- Business Interpretation of Results

---

## Key Insights
- Surge prices increase significantly during high demand and low supply conditions
- Time-of-day and location play a crucial role in surge behavior
- External factors such as weather amplify price fluctuations during peak periods
- Abnormal demand spikes are closely associated with higher surge multipliers
